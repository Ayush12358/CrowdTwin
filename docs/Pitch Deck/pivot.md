# Crowd-Twin: Pitch Strategy Pivot (3 to 4)

Between **Pitch 3** and **Pitch 4**, the presentation has undergone a fundamental shift from a general pitch to a specialized **operational narrative**.

## 1. Strategic Segmentation
*   **From:** Broad targeting (Event Organizers, Insurers, Investors).
*   **To:** Laser-focus on **Security Personnel** (Command Center Operators and Ground Task Forces). Every slide now speaks to the tactical needs of the people physically managing the venue.

## 2. Narrative Headings
*   **From:** Generic labels like *The Problem*, *The Solution*, and *Tech Differentiation*.
*   **To:** **Narrative sentence headings**. Reading only the titles from Slide 2 through Slide 7 forms a logical paragraph that explains the business in a simple, honest flow:
    > *"Event security teams often react to density when it's already too late... We provide a lead time to redirect flow... Our simulation accounts for panic... Designed for command centers... We extend your monitoring... We are seeking partners to pilot."*

## 3. "Honest Delivery" Tone
*   **From:** Dramatization and rhetoric (*"Flying Blind"*, *"The Empire"*, *"The Failure Chain"*).
*   **To:** **Direct and factual language**. Focuses on the physics of the 7 p/m² threshold and the operational advantage of the 15-minute prediction window, removing the "salesy" flair.

## 4. CCTV: From Failure to Asset
*   **From:** Framing CCTV as part of the reason existing systems fail.
*   **To:** Positioned CCTV as a **critical data asset**. Instead of replacing cameras, the pitch explains how Crowd-Twin leverages existing CCTV feeds to add a layer of "proactive future intelligence."

## 5. Research & Tech Framing
*   **Improvement:** Sharpened the technical USP. Clarified the evolution from standard fluid dynamics to a **Psychological ABM** model that simulates how individual "virtual brains" react to stress—moving from modeling water to modeling people.

## 6. Visual Presentation
*   **Rhythm:** Images now appear every 2-3 slides to maintain a steady visual cadence.
*   **Standardization:** Used the `fit` attribute and adjusted background percentages to ensure images are properly framed and no longer "cut off" by the Marp viewport.

## 7. Direct Ask
*   **From:** Macro-market focus on "domination."
*   **To:** Professional **Pilot Ask**, inviting security directors to validate the alpha version in live operational environments.
